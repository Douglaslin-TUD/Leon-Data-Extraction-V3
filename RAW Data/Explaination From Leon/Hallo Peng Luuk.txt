Begin van de toelichting:

 

1W-L

1W-R

etc

These are all "weefstroken", lanes on which vehicles merge onto the highway at the end of a loop immediately before other vehicles leave to go around another loop, creating conflict known as weaving. To reduce the effect of this conflict these lanes tend to be long (on our network), but in a typical cloverleaf interchange these lanes are critically short.

 

1I-L

1I-R

2I-L

2I-R

etc

These are all "invoegstroken". These lanes are identical to acceleration lanes that allow traffic entering a highway to accelerate to the speed of through traffic before merging with it.

 

1U-L

1U-R

2U-L

2U-R

etc

These are all "uitvoegstroken". These lanes are identical to deceleration lanes. A deceleration lane is a lane adjacent to the primary road or street used to improve traffic safety by allowing drivers to pull out of the through lane and decelerate before turning off a surface street or exiting a highway or motorway. 

 

1Q-L

1Q-R

2Q-L

2Q-R

etc

These are all "spitsstroken". These lanes are only available to the traffic during rush hours. They are always on the right side of the carriage way. The lanes that we used to refer to as “plusstroken” are now also called “spitsstroken”. Plusstroken were (and are) situated on the left side of the carriage way. They are usually referred to as lane 1 (1R-L or 1R-R).

 

I will come with more background on the difference between the deceleration and acceleration lanes on the one hand and on-ramps/entrance ramps and off-ramps/exit ramps on the other hand.
